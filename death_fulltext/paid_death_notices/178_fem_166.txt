
        ROSSKOPF-Frances M. (Betty). Died on October 25, 2001, at her home in Jackson, California. A longtime resident of Manhattan, Hastings-on-Hudson, and Ridgefield, Connecticut, she spent most of her later years in Sarasota, Florida. She was the widow of mathematics educator Myron F. Rosskopf. Born and raised in Fairmont, Minnesota, Betty lived in many places. She taught in oneroom schoolhouses in Montana, Minnesota, and the Dakotas. She moved to Missouri during the Depression, where she and her husband had their first home. During World War II, she served as a park ranger in Texas, where her husband was stationed. After the war, they moved to Cazenovia, New York, and thence to New York City and environs. She lived in Oslo, Norway, and Simla, India for brief periods. For as long as possible, Betty spent summers at her beloved island in Canada. Betty studied bonsai with the late Yuji Yoshimura, eventually becoming an exhibitor and teaching associate. She loved the natural world and worked to preserve it. She loved to travel, garden, fish, go out in the boat, entertain. She loved to read, play the piano, listen to jazz and classical music, attend concerts and parties, to dance. She was an avid tennis player. She was a good friend. Betty leaves behind her daughter Kathy and son Peter, their families, and many friends. Donations in her memory may be made to The Riverside Church, 490 Riverside Drive, New York, NY 10027, where her remains rest.
