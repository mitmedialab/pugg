
        METZGER--Bruce L., D.D.S., 65, of Morganville, New Jersey, passed away on Friday, June 8, 2007 at Riverview Medical Center. He was born and grew up in Brooklyn, New York. He obtained a B.A. in history from Brooklyn College and received his D.D.S. from NYU School of Dentistry. After graduating from dental school, he served as a Captain in the U.S. Army and Chief of Oral Surgery at Fort Meade, Maryland, during the Vietnam War. He lived in Morganville for 38 years, where he started and developed a thriving dental practice and raised four children with Miriam, his wife of 44 years. Dr. Metzger held many leadership roles in his community. He served as Town Councilman for Marlboro Township through the mid-1990s. He was Vice President of Jewish Federation of Western Monmouth County. Dr. Metzger was a founding member of the Dental Staff at Centrastate Hospital and served as President of the Dental Society of Monmouth Ocean County. He was also active in raising money for many causes, including Israeli Bonds, United Jewish Appeal and The Chabad House of Western Monmouth County. Dr. Metzger was instrumental in starting the Maimonides Society. He was predeceased by his father, David Metzger, and his father-in-law Philip Fox. He is survived by his wife, Miriam, and his four children: Dr. Scott Metzger and his wife Cheryl; Jodi Pasternak and her husband Marc; Dr. Cory Metzger and her husband Mark Lebovitch; and Daniella and her husband Dr. Michael Adler. Dr. Metzger is also survived by eight grandchildren: Jake, Ben, Mindy, Sam, Max, Olivia, Chloe, and Eden. He is also survived by his mother, Anna Metzger, brother Steven Metzger and his motherin-law, Ruth Fox. The funeral will be held at Marlboro Jewish Center, at 9:30 a.m. on Sunday morning.
