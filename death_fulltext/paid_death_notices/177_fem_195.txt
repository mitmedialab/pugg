
        CLOUGH-Rosa Trillo. Passed away in West Palm Beach June 28, 2001. Born 1906 in New York City, B.A. in Italian from Hunter College, M.A. and a Ph.D. in Italian from Columbia University. Professor of Italian at Hunter College from 1928 to 1956, headed Modern Language Department of Finch College 1956-1971. She founded the Center for Italian Studies in the Palm Beaches, Florida, and was Director until the end of 2000. She authored many articles on Italian art, literature, and culture and published 11 books, including a definitive Italian grammar, major works on Futurism, and Italian art history. Dr. Clough was knighted in 1972 and 1996 by the Italian government, receiving the title of ''Commendatore, Ordine al Merito della Repubblica Italiana.'' In 1995, she was elected to the Hunter College Hall of Fame. She was an educator with boundless energy whose accomplishments are evident in the success of her students. She is mourned by many friends, by her two sons, Shepard A. Clough of Lexington, MA., and Peter N. Clough of Paris, and their families, by a nephew Salvatore Trillo of Glendora, CA, by her cousins, and by her seven grandchildren and thirteen great-grandchildren. A memorial service is planned at the Ann Norton Sculpture Gardens in West Palm Beach on December 1. Tax deductible donations may be made to the Rosa T. Clough Memorial Scholarship Fund, C/O Paul Clough, Comins & Newbury, 84 State Street, Boston, MA 02109.
