
        FITZGEORGE--Harold J., 82, Korean Conflict hero, retired Chairman of Pennzoil Exploration and Production Company, died June 14 at his home in Vero Beach, Florida from complications of Parkinson's disease. Mr. Fitzgeorge (often known as Fitz) spent 29 years with Mobil Oil. Mr. Fitzgeorge served in the US Marine Corps in both World War II and the Korean Conflict. During his service in Korea, Fitz was a member of H Company, 3rd Battalion, 7th Regiment, 1st Marine Corps Division. He received a Purple Heart for being wounded at the battle of Chosin Reservoir and received the Bronze Star for valor for his action under fire. Harold James Fitzgeorge was born in Trenton, N.J. on June 15, 1924. He attended Rutgers and after the war, enrolled at Princeton University, graduating in 1948 with a Degree in Geology. After his service in Korea, Mr. Fitzgeorge worked as a field geologist and manger for Magnolia Petroleum which later became Mobil Oil. He received his M.B.A. from MIT in 1964. Mr. Fitzgeorge held senior positions with Mobil Oil Canada, where he played a major role in the development of the Alaska Pipeline, as well as serving as President of Mobil Oil Venezuela. In 1978, Mr. Fitzgeorge joined Pennzoil as President of Pennzoil Exploration and Production Company. He became chairman in 1981. He retired in 1984. Harold Fitzgeorge married Elizabeth (Bette) Weidel in 1945. (Bette Fitzgeorge died in 1987.) He is survived by the families of his four daughters, Barbara Carr of Norwalk, CT, Virginia Fisher of New Canaan, CT, Patricia Dunning of New York, and Elizabeth Brown of Charlottesville, VA, and his nine grandchildren. In 1999, he married Roberta Tefft of Vero Beach, Florida. A memorial service is planned for Saturday, June 23 at St. Aloysius Church in New Canaan, CT. The family requests that donations be made to the Michael J. Fox Foundation for Parkinson's Research or the Harold J. Fitzgeorge Student Aid Fund, Princeton University.
