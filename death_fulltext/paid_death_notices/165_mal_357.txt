
        GLUCK-Norman E. On October 20, at age 86, in Rio Rancho, New Mexico, after a brief illness fought with courage, dignity and clarity of mind. Loving and devoted husband of the late Rose B. Dear friend, companion and golf partner of Katie Holzer. Devoted and caring brother of Helen, the late David and the late Etta Ungerfeld and brother-in-law of Shirley B. Krass. Much cherished and respected uncle of Joann Rollnick, Ellen Krass and Stephen and Sallie Krass; great-uncle of Kathy and Gerald Berenstein, Nancy and Lawrence Jacobson, Stephanie and William Hepner, Caroline and Adam Levy and Jamie Krass; and great great-uncle of Samantha, Chelsea, Jamie, Benjamin, Jennifer and Jason. Norman, a consummate gentleman, touched the lives of many people, each of whom is privileged to have known him and to have benefited from his guidance, intelligence and wisdom. He was a very special person and will be greatly missed. Contributions in Norman's memory may be made to a charity of your choice.
        GLUCK-Norman. The Officers, Governors and Members of the New York Friars Club mourn the loss of their esteemed Member Friar Norman Gluck. A Member of The Friars Club for four decades, he was fierce in his dedication to excellence and continued support of humanitarian causes. We are deeply indepted to the personal guidance he provided us while serving as a Member of The Board of Governors. Private services were held in Rio Racho, New Mexico. Alan King, Abbot Freddie Roman, Dean Sally Jessy Raphael, Prior David B. Cornstein, Treasurer Stewie Stone, Scribe Jean-Pierre Trebot, Executive Director
