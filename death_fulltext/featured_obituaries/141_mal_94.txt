
        Randolph Carter, an author who wrote several plays produced on  Broadway, died on Oct. 12 at St. Albans Naval Hospital in Queens. He was 90.
        Mr. Carter's first Broadway production was ''Arms of Venus'' (1937), based on Petronius's ''Satyricon.'' Variety called it ''a tempest in a Roman tomb'' and ''an orgy of laughs.''
        Mr. Carter was associated with J. J. Shubert for 10 years, serving as a play doctor and providing dialogue for musicals and operettas including ''The Student Prince,'' ''Countess Maritza,'' ''The Land of Smiles'' and ''The Merry Widow.''
        Other Broadway productions included a dramatization of ''Wuthering Heights'' in 1940 and ''Eugenia,'' starring Tallulah Bankhead, in 1957. His ''Florence Nightingale,'' starring Katharine Cornell, was presented on radio in 1951 by the Theater Guild on the Air.
        He also adapted ''Wuthering Heights'' for a television production that starred Richard Burton and Claire Bloom.
        In 1984 his adaptation of James T. Farrell's ''Death of Nora Ryan'' was presented Off Broadway.
        From 1966 to 1969 Mr. Carter was the drama critic on the John Wingate news program on WOR-TV.
        He was the author of ''The World of Flo Ziegfeld'' (1974) and a monograph, ''Joseph Urban'' (1992).
