        Alex L. Hart, a transportation consultant who in the 1960's was executive director of the New York-New Jersey Transportation Agency, died March 11 in Aurora, Colo. He was 73 years old and was a former resident of Centerport, L.I.
        In the 1950's, Mr. Hart was chief of airport planning for the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey and guided the expansion of La Guardia and Kennedy International Airports.
        He was born in Export, Pa., and was a graduate of Pennsylvania State University. He received a doctorate in statistics and economics from the University of Minnesota.
        He is survived by his wife, Joyce, of Playa del Rey, Calif.; three daughters, Carolinn Rahman of Aurora, Cathy Kumley of Aurora, and Betsy Katz, of Allendale, N.J.; two sons, Walter, of Lloyd Harbor, L.I., and Joe, of Mission Viejo, Calif., and a sister, Charlotte Hutcheson, who lives in Pennsylvania.
