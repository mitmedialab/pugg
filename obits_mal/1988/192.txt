        Donald L. Rogers, president of the Association of Bank Holding Companies, died Jan. 18 of colon cancer at his home in Washington. He was 59.
        A native of East Steubenville, W.Va., Mr. Rogers graduated from Miami University in Oxford, Ohio, and received a law degree from Ohio State University. In 1955, he was appointed Counsel to the United States Senate Banking Committee. He joined the Association of Bank Holding Companies in 1958.
        Survivors include his wife, Helen.
