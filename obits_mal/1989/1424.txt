        Arnold Bank, chairman of the department of neurological and sensory sciences at Albert Einstein Medical Center in Philadelphia, died of leukemia Sunday at the center. He was 58 years old and lived in Elkins Park, Pa.
        Dr. Bank joined the center in 1967 and in 1969 became a department head. He taught at Temple University Medical School.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from Syracuse University and Albany Medical College of Union University in Albany.
        Dr. Bank is survived by his wife, the former Joan Lavine; his mother, Frances Bank of the Bronx; a sister, Sondra Scharf of Manhattan; and three children, Andrew of Boston, Melissa of Ithaca, N.Y., and Margery, of Manhattan.
