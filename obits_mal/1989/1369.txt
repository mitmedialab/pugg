        Magnus Jan Krynski, professor emeritus of Slavic languages and literature at Duke University, Durham, N.C., died of a heart attack Thursday at Durham County General Hospital. He was 67 years old and lived in Durham.
        Born in Warsaw, Dr. Krynski left Poland in 1939 and came to the United States in 1948. He received a Ph.D. from Columbia University in 1962. He taught briefly at Duke and several other universities before becoming chairman of the department of Slavic languages at Duke in 1966. He retired in 1987.
        He was a specialist in 20th century Polish literature and translated three volumes of poetry into English. He was chairman of the North Carolina Ethnic Voters for Reagan-Bush '84 and was also active on behalf of the Solidarity movement in Poland.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth.
